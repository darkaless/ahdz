REBORN

WEEK 1, DAY 1, SELF PORTRAIT
APRIL 11, 2016

I decided to start a new project. This 365 project will make me pick up my camera every single day and photography something. I have divided each week into a special theme, which will go around all week long. I am beyond excited about this. It’s been so long since I actually brainstorm and induce myself into creative thinking… But I love the excitement, and the rush it brings me to do this.

Don’t get me wrong. I like photographing events and ventures… But, it is not what I love. Ever since I was a young girl, I fell in love with art. I started sketching and drawing as an attempt to understand the feel of the world that surrounded me. When I was around seventeen, my brother got a camera, he wanted to become a photographer. At the time, I had no knowledge of how to take a photograph on manual mode. After a year or so, he lost interest in photography and I picked up the camera and ventured on exploring the world of photography.

Oh! What a great change it was! I don’t think I can express into words what photography means to me. I was in a very bad place when I decided to try out photography. But it gave meaning to my life again. Maybe it sounds crazy to you, but it gave me a purpose what I had none. It took my mind to magical places in which everything was possible. And it still is.

Anyway, as I was thinking on today’s self-portrait. I realized that I needed to feel like that eighteen year old self who believed anything was possible. Sometimes, as grownups, we forget of the important things in life because we focus on our 9 to 5 jobs and its related problems. We get so stressed over meaningless things that we don’t even realize the value of time until it is too late. I spent a year like that and feel like I have lost an era of potential growth. I can’t imagine what it must feel like to lose your whole life without doing what you love. I felt like I had lost a part of myself this past year… I had lost my hunger for creating and capturing the ideas that run through my mind. I stopped listening to myself and just got carried away with daily events and activities. There have been highlights, I know that, and I am eternally grateful for the friends that came out of this and to my husband, who has been my support me all this year. But now, I feel like myself again. These past few weeks, have felt like a breath of fresh air for me. I can’t even sleep at night because I am so excited to face head on the next day.

I found myself again. And that is what this self-portrait means to me. It marks the beginning of great things that will come. Because I know that if you do what you love with passion and persistence, great things will come along. I can’t wait for more… Tomorrow will always be an adventure to me

Sincerely, Katia A. Hernandez
