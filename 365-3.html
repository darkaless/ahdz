WEEK 1, DAY 3, SELF PORTRAIT
APRIL 13, 2016

Today I decided to do a continuation of my first self- portrait (see it here) It makes me feel in that state of mind in which anything is possible… I like keeping myself in that frequency. Not only does it make me want to do more things all the time, but it also make me be happier for longer periods of time.

I used to be very depressed and angry all the time. I think my high school classmates can vouch for that (I’m sorry guys!) My mother had no idea of what to do with me anymore. And I wasn’t doing crazy things all the time, I was just…. Lonely, I guess. I spent my days reading, painting, listening to depressive music and cutting myself. That was a regular day. One of my classmates, Daniella, asked me something that has been with me since then. She asked, “Who hurt you so bad for you to be like this?” But the truth is, no one hurt me. I had everything. My family was not perfect but I had one. I have two loving older brothers who watched my back all along and two awesome parents who gave their best every day for us. In spite of that, I was still depressed. I still don’t understand how that happened but it did. I remember I used to wear an oversized black sweater to hide my scars. If you didn’t know, I grew up in San Pedro Sula. Over there, it’s like 40 degrees Celsius every day! Can you imagine wearing a sweater all day long!? I was crazy.

Things got better after high school. I met new people, discovered my love for photography, fashion, fitness and I learned to move on. Photography took me out of that hole, along with the help of my best friend and now husband. I know it sounds cheesy, hell, maybe it is. But that’s okay, it’s the way things turned out for me.

This picture takes me back to those dark times, and how an alternate reality in which anything and everything is possible helped me to find myself. To me, this portrait reminds me of the time in which I finally decided to let go of all those negative vibes. I remember fleeing like the world around me was coming to life with colors.

Change isn’t easy… It takes time. And although I still struggle with feeling depressed, I chose to be happy every day. I won’t lie to you, there is no permanent pill that will make you feel well all the time, but you can be if you want to. You must only decide.

Now, enough about sappy stories. I hope you like this portrait as much as I do. I will share with you one last thing… A piece of a poem that I think, describes my portrait. Written by Sonny Carroll:

“Her senses now heightened, she sees everything so clearly. She hears the wind rustling through the trees; beckoning her to live the dreams she holds so dearly. She feels the softness of her hands and muses at the strength that they possess. Her needs and desires she has learned to express. She has tasted the bitter and savored the sweet fruits of life, overcome adversity and pushed past heartache and strife.”

Sincerely, Katia A. Hernandez
